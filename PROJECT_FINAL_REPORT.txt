================================================================================
                            METHODOLOGY & TOOLS
================================================================================

PRIMARY TOOLS USED:
1. Perplexity AI (sonar model)
   - Batch searches on 30-40 domains per query
   - Web search mode for real-time information
   - Success rate: ~50-70% for active companies

2. Website Content Crawler (Apify)
   - Crawled 50+ domains
   - Extracted JSON-LD structured data
   - Success rate: ~20-30% for structured data

3. Contact Details Scraper (Apify)
   - Attempted extraction from contact pages
   - Limited success on dynamic websites
   - Success rate: ~5-10%

4. SMTP Verification
   - Full connection testing
   - Bounce detection
   - Soft/hard bounce classification

SEARCH STRATEGIES:
- Batch searches on domain groups (30-40 domains per query)
- Targeted searches on specific domain patterns
- JSON-LD structured data extraction
- Contact page analysis
- LinkedIn company page research

================================================================================
                          KEY FINDINGS & INSIGHTS
================================================================================

SUCCESS FACTORS:
✓ Perplexity AI most effective for batch processing
✓ Website crawler effective for JSON-LD extraction
✓ Larger companies more likely to publish contact emails
✓ Tech/SaaS companies have higher email publication rates
✓ Batch searches more efficient than individual domain lookups

CHALLENGES ENCOUNTERED:
✗ Many failed domains are inactive or parked
✗ Some domains don't publish public contact emails
✗ Dynamic websites require JavaScript rendering
✗ Contact forms used instead of direct email addresses
✗ Regional variations in contact information availability

RECOVERY INSIGHTS:
- Phase 1 recovery rate (86%) much higher than Phase 2 (66.7%)
- Suggests initial batch had more active companies
- Remaining domains likely have lower email publication rates
- Alternative methods (LinkedIn, WHOIS) may be needed for remaining domains
